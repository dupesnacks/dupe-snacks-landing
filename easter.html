The Easter Basket Formula (works every time)

1 Large Piece + 3 Medium + 5 Small + 1 Activity

💎 Large: Chocolate bunny ($5-7)

🍬 Small: Jelly beans, gummies, single-serve candies ($1-3 each)

Budget Breakdown

💰 Budget Basket ($20-25): Mix of small/medium items from our list
💰 Mid-Range ($35-50): Include 1 large chocolate bunny + mix

Pro Tips for Gluten-Free Easter Baskets

  • ✅ Check EVERY item — brands change formulas frequently
  • ✅ Mix textures — chocolate, gummies, candy for variety
  • ✅ Include non-candy items — temporary tattoos, plastic eggs, toys
  • ✅ Get it early — popular GF items sell out pre-Easter
  • ✅ Verify certifications — look for "Certified Gluten-Free" labels

More About Gluten-Free Easter

What makes Easter challenging for gluten-free families? Traditional Easter candy — Reese's, Cadbury, Cadbury Eggs, Peeps, and most jellybeans — contains gluten or has been manufactured in facilities with cross-contamination risks. For kids with celiac disease, even trace amounts of gluten can cause serious health issues.

Are these alternatives as good? Yes. In many cases, the gluten-free dupes are actually better quality, made with premium ingredients, and taste superior to the originals.
